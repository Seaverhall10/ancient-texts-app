        "definition": "Alternate transliteration of mal'akhim. Plural of malakh, meaning 'messengers' or 'angels.' The term derives from the root l-'-k ('to send'), indicating one sent with a message or mission. Malaakhim can refer to human messengers (Gen 32:3; Num 20:14) or divine beings—angels who serve in God's heavenly court and execute His will on earth. Angels appear throughout Genesis and Exodus as agents of divine communication and action: announcing births (Gen 18), destroying Sodom (Gen 19), protecting Isaac (Gen 22:11), wrestling with Jacob (Gen 32:24-30), and leading Israel through the wilderness
