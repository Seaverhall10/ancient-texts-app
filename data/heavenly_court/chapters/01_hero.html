How to Read This Study

A Scripture consistently depicts Yahweh presiding over a populated heavenly court of created spiritual beings (Job 1–2; Psalm 82; 1 Kings 22; Daniel 7).

A The Bible records multiple distinct rebellions: the Edenic adversary (Genesis 3), the Watchers who took human wives (Genesis 6; Jude 6; 2 Peter 2:4), and corrupt territorial powers over the nations (Deuteronomy 32; Psalm 82; Daniel 10).

A Jesus and the apostles directly engage this unseen conflict through exorcism, authority claims, and cosmic-victory language (the Gospels; Colossians 2:15; Ephesians 6:12).

A The biblical end-state is not disembodied souls escaping to “Heaven” but a new heaven and new earth with God dwelling bodily among resurrected humanity (Isaiah 65–66; Revelation 21–22).

Evidence labels used throughout: A = directly stated in canonical Scripture · B = strong inference from biblical data, or supported by Second Temple Jewish literature (1 Enoch, Jubilees, Dead Sea Scrolls) · C = supported by ancient Near Eastern texts, archaeology, classical sources, or cross-cultural parallels
