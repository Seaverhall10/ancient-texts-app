## PART 3 — THE PROPHETS AS COVENANT PROSECUTORS

### The Riv Framework
- Hebrew רִיב — riv — covenant lawsuit, legal proceeding
- When Yahweh has a riv with Israel he is prosecuting a case against a covenant partner who broke treaty
- Micah 6:1-2 — mountains and foundations of earth summoned as WITNESSES — same witnesses at covenant ratification
- Deuteronomy 32:1 — Moses calls heaven and earth as witnesses to the covenant
- "Thus says Yahweh" — koh amar Yahweh — royal messenger protocol, exact formula used by ANE royal messengers
- Prophets are official royal messengers of the divine king, not inspired commentators

SIX-PART RIV STRUCTURE (consistent across prophetic books, mirrors Hittite treaty lawsuit documents):
1. Call to witnesses — heaven, earth, mountains summoned
2. Statement of relationship — "I brought you out of Egypt, I gave you the land"
3. Accusation — specific covenant violations listed
4. Evidence — covenant failures documented
5. Verdict — judgment declared
6. Offer of restoration — even in judgment, a way back

### Isaiah
- Two major movements mirroring riv at massive scale:
- Chapters 1-39 = PROSECUTION. Israel and nations indicted. Evidence of covenant failure. Judgment declared. Assyria as Yahweh's instrument.
- Chapters 40-66 = RESTORATION. "Comfort, comfort my people." Case prosecuted, sentence served, restoration promised.
- Servant Songs (42, 49, 50, 52-53) = the hinge — the one who bears the covenant curse so the people receive the covenant blessing
- Isaiah 40:1-2 — "her warfare is ended, her iniquity is pardoned" — legal declaration, sentence served, case closed
- Isaiah 44:6, 48:12 — "I am the first and I am the last" — same speaker as Revelation 1:8 (Jesus)

### Jeremiah
- Most emotionally raw prophet — prosecuting a people he loves, watching them walk toward destruction
- Jeremiah 7:16 — Yahweh forbids Jeremiah from even praying for the people — verdict already set
- Jeremiah 20:7 — "you deceived me, Yahweh, and I was deceived" — the cost of being covenant attorney
- Jeremiah 31:31-34 — New Covenant prophecy = ultimate restoration offer after full prosecution
- The prophet's anguish = model for honest prayer, not sanitized ministry

### Ezekiel
- Most cosmic of the three — sees the divine council (merkavah) and prosecutes from heavenly vantage point

- Cherubim to threshold, threshold to east gate, east gate to mountain east of the city

### The Prophets We Don't Have
- The Bible references prophets whose writings are lost:
  - 1 Chronicles 29:29 — Samuel the seer, Nathan the prophet, Gad the seer — all written, none preserved
  - 2 Chronicles 9:29 — Ahijah the Shilonite, Iddo the seer — gone
  - 2 Chronicles 12:15 — Shemaiah the prophet, Iddo — gone

### Who He Is

### His Name — Full Hebrew Analysis
מַלְכִּי־צֶדֶק — Malkhi-Tzedeq

MELEK (מֶלֶךְ) = king. The -i suffix = possessive. "My king" or "king of"
TZEDEQ (צֶדֶק) = righteousness, justice, equity, conformity to the standard of right
- Root of tzaddik (righteous one), tzedakah (righteousness/charity)
- Used for Yahweh himself — Genesis 18:25 "shall not the judge of all the earth do tzedeq?"
- Three valid readings: King of Righteousness / My King is Righteousness / Righteousness is King

SALEM (שָׁלֵם) = root of shalom — peace, wholeness, completeness, nothing missing nothing broken
- Almost certainly early Jerusalem — Psalm 76:2 uses Salem and Zion in parallel

COMBINED TITLE: King of Righteousness + King of Peace
- Hebrews 7:2 makes the order deliberate — righteousness FIRST, then peace
- You cannot have genuine shalom without tzedeq
- This is the sequence of the entire biblical narrative — righteous order established, then wholeness

### His Titles
- Priest of El Elyon — אֵל עֶלְיוֹן — God Most High

- Peter quotes it at Pentecost in Acts 2:34
- Hebrews quotes it repeatedly
- "You are a priest forever after the order of Melchizedek" — Yahweh swearing an irrevocable oath

### The Levitical Priesthood Requirements (Contrast)
TRIBAL LINEAGE:
- Numbers 3:10 — must be born a Levite, specifically descended from Aaron for the priesthood
- Any outsider approaching = death
- Genealogical documentation required — Ezra 2:61-63, those without proof suspended from service

AGE:
- Numbers 4:3 — service begins at 30 years old
- Numbers 8:24 — preparatory work from 25
- Mandatory retirement at 50 — Numbers 4:3
- Fixed biological window

PHYSICAL PERFECTION (Leviticus 21:16-23):
13 specific disqualifying conditions including: blind, lame, mutilated face, limb too long, injured foot or hand, hunchback, dwarf, defective sight, itching disease, scabs, crushed testicles
- Could eat holy food but COULD NOT approach the altar or enter the sanctuary

RITUAL PURITY:
- No touching corpses except immediate family
- No marrying divorced woman, widow, or prostitute
- High priest could only marry a virgin from his own people
- Must wash hands and feet in bronze laver before entering sanctuary — "lest they die"
- No physical discharge, skin disease, or emission

GARMENTS (Exodus 28):
- Specific garments in precise detail — ephod, breastpiece with twelve stones, robe with golden bells
- Golden bells on hem — "so that he does not die" when entering holy place
- Gold plate engraved "Holy to Yahweh"
- Without correct garments = no legitimate access

HIGH PRIEST ADDITIONAL:
- Could never leave sanctuary during service
- Could not uncover head or tear clothes — Caiaphas violated this law when he tore his robes at Jesus' trial
- Yom Kippur: bathe entire body, plain white linen, enter Holy of Holies alone, once per year only
- Rope tied to ankle so body could be retrieved if struck dead

SUCCESSION:
- Hereditary — transferred through garments (Numbers 20:25-28, Aaron to Eleazar)

WHY JESUS COULD NOT BE A LEVITICAL PRIEST:
- Jesus was from the tribe of JUDAH — Hebrews 7:13-14 states this explicitly
- "Moses said nothing about priests" in connection with Judah
- Wrong genealogy. Full stop. No amount of righteousness or miracles changes this.
- This is why Hebrews must establish the Melchizedekian order so carefully

### Hebrews' Argument
- Hebrews 7:3 — "without father or mother or genealogy, having neither beginning of days nor end of life, but resembling the Son of God he continues a priest forever"
- NOT saying Melchizedek had no parents — arguing from the text's deliberate silence
- Genesis is meticulous about genealogy — its silence on Melchizedek is theologically intentional
- Hebrews 7:4 — Abraham the patriarch gave him a tithe — the lesser is blessed by the greater
- Hebrews 7:10 — Levi was in the loins of Abraham — the entire Levitical priesthood tithed to Melchizedek
- Hebrews 7:11-12 — if perfection were attainable through Levitical priesthood, no need for another order. Change of priesthood = change of law. New covenant is not Mosaic covenant upgraded.
- Hebrews 7:16 — Jesus qualified not by biological descent but by "the power of an indestructible life"
- Hebrews 7:27 — once for all sacrifice vs. annual Levitical repetition
- Hebrews 10:1 — "the law has but a shadow of the good things to come instead of the true form of these realities"

### The Levitical System as Shadow and Prophecy
- Physical perfection = pointed to resurrected, incorruptible body
- Genealogical requirement = pointed to ontological qualification — what he IS not who he was born to
- Annual Yom Kippur = pointed to once-for-all sacrifice ending the need for repetition
- The entire Levitical system was pedagogical — teaching Israel what the real high priest would look like through contrast and anticipation

### The Bread and Wine
- Genesis 14:18 — Melchizedek brings bread and wine to Abraham
- ONLY pre-Mosaic priestly act involving bread and wine in the entire OT
- Jesus at the Last Supper: bread and wine, "this is my body, this is my blood," inaugurating new covenant
- The priestly order Jesus claims traces back through this act to Genesis 14

### 11QMelchizedek — Dead Sea Scrolls

- Quotes Isaiah 61:1 — "proclaim liberty to captives" — applies to Melchizedek as executor of ultimate jubilee

- With it: the author of Hebrews and Qumran community drawing on the same tradition

## PART 6 — THE CANON HISTORY

### Jerome (4th Century AD)
- Commissioned by Pope Damasus I in 382 AD to produce standardized Latin Bible — the Vulgate
- Preferred Hebrew canon as defined by rabbis at Jamnia after 70 AD temple destruction
- Called deuterocanonical books (Maccabees, Sirach, Tobit, Judith, Wisdom, etc.) second-tier
- Church overruled him — they went into the Vulgate anyway
- His translation choices shaped Western theology for 1,600 years:
  - Genesis 3:15 — rendered with feminine pronoun "she will bruise" (ipsa conteret) instead of Hebrew "he" — foundation of Catholic Mariology, NOT what the Hebrew says
  - Isaiah 7:14 — alma (young woman) rendered virgo (virgin) — follows LXX but obscures Hebrew precision
  - Consistently flattened elohim vocabulary — where Hebrew distinguishes Yahweh from divine council, Jerome often rendered everything as Deus (singular God) — contributed directly to Western church losing divine council framework for a millennium

### Council of Trent (1546 AD)
- Convened in direct response to the Protestant Reformation
- Luther went back to Hebrew rabbinic canon in reaction to Rome
- Luther also questioned James, Hebrews, Jude, Revelation — called James "an epistle of straw"
- Council of Trent formally and dogmatically defined the Catholic canon for the first time with full conciliar authority
- Included the deuterocanonical books Jerome had been uncertain about
- Declared the Vulgate the authoritative text
- Protestant and Catholic canons were BOTH partially defined in reaction to each other in the 16th century

### What This Means
- The canon was not handed down complete from heaven — it was a process
- 1 Enoch did NOT fail a theological test — it failed a political and institutional test centuries after it was written
- The Qumran community treated 1 Enoch as authoritative alongside Isaiah
- Jude treated it as prophecy — stronger endorsement than any council's silence
- Ethiopian Orthodox Church: oldest continuous Christian tradition, traces to Acts 8 (Ethiopian eunuch), 1 Enoch and Jubilees in their canon continuously for 1,700 years — they were not wrong
