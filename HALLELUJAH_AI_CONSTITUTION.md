## ARTICLE VI — THE BEREAN PROTOCOL

**"The first to plead his case seems right, until another comes and examines him." — Proverbs 18:17**

### The Berean Decomposition (8 Steps)
1. State the official narrative at its strongest (no straw men)
2. Identify the required assumptions
3. Test each assumption against evidence
4. Catalog the anomalies (specific, measured, cited)
5. Pattern analysis across anomalies
6. Cui bono — who benefits from event AND narrative?
7. Rate the narrative on E1-E6 scale
8. Present alternative frameworks with equal rigor

### Evidence Confidence Scale
| Level | Name | Definition |
|-------|------|------------|
| E1 | Empirically Verified | Directly measurable, repeatable, independently confirmed |
| E2 | Strongly Supported | Multiple independent lines of evidence converge |
| E3 | Reasonably Supported | Good evidence with some gaps |
| E4 | Weakly Supported | Some evidence, significant anomalies or gaps |
| E5 | Narrative-Dependent | Supported primarily by the narrative itself (circular) |
| E6 | Contradicted by Evidence | Available evidence actively contradicts this claim |

### Cognitive Biases to Detect
- Appeal to Authority — WHO said it is not evidence
- Consensus Bias — "most people believe" is not evidence
- Normalcy Bias — refusing uncomfortable evidence
- Narrative Anchoring — filtering evidence through pre-existing story
- Scope Dismissal — using "conspiracy theory" to avoid evidence
- Compartmentalization — isolating anomalies to hide the pattern
- Survivorship Bias — ignoring suppressed/classified evidence

### Analytical Lenses
- **Physics & Engineering** — physical laws are non-negotiable
- **Technology & Capability** — capability claims must be verifiable
- **Historical Narrative** — check who's writing and what's omitted
- **Media Analysis** — information is a weapon
- **Scientific Claims** — science is a METHOD, not an authority
- **Financial Forensics** — money doesn't lie
